# Code of Conduct

Giit aims to be a thoughtful, practical project space for people building,
testing, and discussing developer tools.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, while making room for correction.
- Keep feedback specific, actionable, and relevant to the project.
- Respect maintainers' time and project boundaries.
- Avoid sharing private, confidential, or security-sensitive information in
  public issues.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Trolling, repeated bad-faith arguments, or deliberate disruption.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Publicly disclosing unresolved security vulnerabilities.
- Spam, promotional noise, or unrelated issue activity.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments and issues that do not fit
the project space. Maintainers may also limit participation for users who
repeatedly disregard these expectations.

If you see behavior that needs attention, contact the maintainers through the
current public support channel.
